This week we've taken a look at the environmental impact of ZipLoc Bags and dicovered that though convenient, single-use plastic bags are contaminating our waterways and living in our landfills for hundreds of years. While Stasher bags offer an excellent sustainable replacement, the upfront cost can add up quickly.

If a collection of Stasher bags isn't really in the budget right now, or you're just feeling crafty, you can make this resuable beeswax wrap sandwich bag instead! I made some before my husband and I took a cross country road trip to Utah and we would both happy with how well they kept our PB & J's when we brought them on our hikes.

What You'll Need:

  • 7" x 18" Beeswax Wrap
  • Button
  • Elastic
  • Sewing notions
  1. {% include image-source-set.html image="36-ziplocs/1-make-beeswax-wrap.jpg" alt="rectangular piece of polka dot fabric" %}

    Follow this tutorial to make a 7" x 18" beeswax wrap.

  2. {% include image-source-set.html image="36-ziplocs/2-sew-edges.jpg" alt="wrong side of polka dot fabric with edge folded over and sewn" %}

    Stich 1/2" of fabric down on each 7" side of your beeswax wrap.

  3. {% include image-source-set.html image="36-ziplocs/3-fold-over.jpg" alt="fabric right side up with wrong side flap folded over 7 inches" %}

    With your fabric right side up, fold one side over 7".

  4. {% include image-source-set.html image="36-ziplocs/4-fold-back.jpg" alt="fabric folded back over so only right side is visible" %}

    Fold this same flap back the other way 1/2". See picture.

  5. {% include image-source-set.html image="36-ziplocs/5-fold-again.jpg" alt="fabric folded a third time, showing accordian effect" %}

    Fold the same flap another 1/2", so that it lines up with the initial fold. See picture.

  6. {% include image-source-set.html image="36-ziplocs/6-sew-seams.jpg" alt="edges of fabric stitch closed" %}

    Stitch each side down with a 1/2"seam allowance.

  7. {% include image-source-set.html image="36-ziplocs/7-flip-inside-out.jpg" alt="sewn fabric flipped right side out to show seam" %}

    Flip your bag inside out. You should have a nice square bottom, perfect for sandwiches.

  8. {% include image-source-set.html image="36-ziplocs/8-finish-edges.jpg" alt="edges of top flap folded over and stitched shut" %}

    Fold over the edges of the top flap 1/2" and stich in place.

  9. {% include image-source-set.html image="36-ziplocs/9-attach-elastic.jpg" alt="elastic loop attached to top edge of fabric" %}

    Add a small elastic loop to the edge of the top flap.

  10. {% include image-source-set.html image="36-ziplocs/10-attach-button.jpg" alt="button sewn on front of bag" %}

    Fold the flap over to determine a good spot to attach your button.

  11. {% include image-source-set.html image="36-ziplocs/11-finished-baggie.jpg" alt="hand holding complete sandwich bag" %}

    Step back and admire your homemade sandwich bag.
