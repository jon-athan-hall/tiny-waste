It doesn't take much to make your own hankie and by keeping one on you, you'll have no need to waste a paper napkin when you're out and about. Note: this tutorial is only for the napkin itself, not a lesson on embroidery.

What You'll Need:

  • Fabric remnants/old pillowcase/tired clothes - you can use anything, but natural fabrics are typically more absorbent.
  • Scissors
  • Needle and Thread/Sew Machine and thread to match fabric
  • Straight Pins
  • Iron

Let's Make a Hankie!

  1. 1. Grab your fabric. I used a stained linen pillowcase that my sister was getting rid of.

  2. 2. Cut out a square. You can make it any size you like, but I cut out 12" x 12" and found it to be perfect.

  3. 3. You can fold your square diagonally to make sure it is a nice even square.

  4. 4. Fold over each edge about a quarter of an inch and iron.

  5. 5. Snip off a small triangle from each corner. See picture.

  6. 6. Fold down each corner about a quarter of an inch from point.

  7. 7. Fold each edge over a quarter of an inch and pin in place. Corners should meet at a clean 45 degree seam.

  8. 8. Iron your new folds to set the crease.

  9. 9. Sew down all four sides by hand or machine. You should be able to use the edge of the sewing machine foot as a guide.

  10. 10. Your hankie is complete. Embellish however you like! I embroidered a little floral "A" for my first name.
