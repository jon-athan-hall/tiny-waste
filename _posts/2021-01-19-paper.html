It's going to take a few months for that junk mail to stop taking over my mailbox. Until then, I'm going to use it to make some cute paper.

What you'll need:

  • Junk mail
  • Window screen or flat strainer - I use an actual full window screen that I found in my garage. You can use the screen from an busted old window and staple it to a picture frame. A large flat kitchen strainer woul also work, but will produce smaller round pieces of paper.
  • Blender - you can usually find a cheap used one at second hand shops.
  • Water catch - a tub or baking sheet that is ideally bigger than your screen.
  • Towel - for blotting excess water.
  • Scoop or spoon - completely optional, your hands will work just as well.
  • Flower petals and leaves - also optional. You can add any kind of flair to your paper or none at all.

  1. 1. Gather you junk mail. You can use old bills, coupons, magazines, advertisements, credit card offers (minus the plastic credit card).

  2. 2. Remove all of the plastic windows from envelopes.

  3. 3. Shred or rip up the paper into small pieces and put them in a blender. Fill to top.

  4. 4. Pour water in the blender, just enough to cover the paper strips.

  5. 5. Blend until paper becomes a mushy pulp and all large pieces are gone.

  6. 6. Set your screen over something to catch the excess water. I used the lid of plastic storage bin.

  7. 7. This is an optional step, but if you would like to add a little extra flair, now is the time to do it. You can put flower petals, leaves, or whatever you want to expirement with directly on the screen.

  8. 8. Scoop the paper pulp out of the blender and plop it on your screen. I used a small kitchen strainer so that I could leave some of the water behind.

  9. 9. Spread the pulp out on the screen as thin as you can without creating holes. Use a towel to pat the pulp and soak up excess water. Leave it to dry.

  10. 10. Once the paper is completely dry, which might take a few days, slowly and carefully peel it off of the screen.

  11. 11. Cut the paper into desired size. I like the look of the rough edges, so instead of using scissors, I simply fold the paper and carefully pull it apart at the fold.

  12. 12. You can use this paper to make cards, to-do lists, labels...be creative!

  13. 13. Now you know how to make cute, zero waste cards and stationary!
