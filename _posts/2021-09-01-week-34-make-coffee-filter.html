What You'll Need:

  • Unbleached Cotton Fabric
  • Serger or Sewing Machine
  • Scissors
  • Pattern- it's free! Click to download.
  • Chalk or something to trace pattern onto fabric
  1. {% include image-source-set.html image="34-coffee-tea/1-trace-pattern.jpg" alt="Piece of cotton fabric with shield shape patterns chalked on it - the shields are medieval style, not captain america" %}

    Using the free pattern I've attached above, trace 8 shield shapes onto your fabric.

  2. {% include image-source-set.html image="34-coffee-tea/2-cut-pattern.jpg" alt="fabric cut into 8 shield shape pieces" %}

    Cut out all 8 shapes.

  3. {% include image-source-set.html image="34-coffee-tea/3-double-layers.jpg" alt="four stacks of two shield shape fabric pieces" %}

    This will be a double layer filter, so put your cut pieces in stacks of two.

  4. {% include image-source-set.html image="34-coffee-tea/4-stack-four.jpg" alt="hand holding four shield shape fabric pieces together" %}

    Line up two sets together, you should have four identical pieces.

  5. {% include image-source-set.html image="34-coffee-tea/5-serge-one-side.jpg" alt="shield shape fabric piece serged on one of the curved sides" %}

    Serge or zig zag stich one curved side of the stack.

  6. {% include image-source-set.html image="34-coffee-tea/6-two-sets.jpg" alt="two sets of shield shape fabric pieces that have been sewn together" %}

    Repeat steps 4 and 5 with the other cut pieces.

  7. {% include image-source-set.html image="34-coffee-tea/7-stack-sides.jpg" alt="" %}

    Line up the curved edges of your two sewn pieces. See picture.

  8. {% include image-source-set.html image="34-coffee-tea/8-serge-together.jpg" alt="" %}

    Serge or zig zag stitch the curved edges together. It should have a bowl shape when you are finished.

  9. {% include image-source-set.html image="34-coffee-tea/9-serge-edges.jpg" alt="" %}

    Finish off the straight edges with a serger or a zig zag stitch.

How to use your coffee filter

First, wash and dry your filter. Use it the same way you would a normal filter, only after the coffee is finished, rinse until the water runs clear and hang to dry.
