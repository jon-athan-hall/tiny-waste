It's always best to use what you have, but if you need a travel mug, here is a look at the pros and cons of some of the most popular options.

Glass - Keep Cup

{% include image-source-set.html image="7-disposable-cups/glass-reusable.jpg" alt="glass travel mug with cork coozi and silicone lid" %}

Pros

  • Microwaveable
  • Dishwasher Safe
  • Recyclable
  • Comes in compact sizes, better for coffee machines

Cons

  • Most Breakable
  • Can be hot to the touch

Stainless Steel - Hyrdroflask

{% include image-source-set.html image="7-disposable-cups/stainless-reusable.jpg" alt="Grey hydroflask travel mug" %}

Pros

  • Durable
  • Keeps drinks hot/cold for hours
  • Leak proof

Cons

  • Not microwable
  • Not usually dishwasher safe
  • More expensive than other options

Ceramic - Ello

{% include image-source-set.html image="7-disposable-cups/ceramic-reusable.jpg" alt="white ceramic travel mug with handle" %}

Pros

  • No added taste to beverages
  • Most like a regular coffee mug
  • Usually microwave-safe

Cons

  • Breakable
  • Not always dishwasher-safe
  • Can be hot to the touch

Silicone - Gosili

{% include image-source-set.html image="7-disposable-cups/silicone-reusable.jpg" alt="grey silicone travel mug" %}

Pros

  • Microwave-safe
  • Dishwasher-safe
  • Lightweight

Cons

  • Can be hot to the touch
  • Can slightly alter the taste of your beverage
  • Doesn't keep heat as long as other options

Plastic - Starbucks

{% include image-source-set.html image="7-disposable-cups/plastic-mug.jpg" alt="XX" %}

Pros

  • Inexpensive
  • Durable
  • Dishwasher-safe

Cons

  • Not biodegradable
  • Plastic leeches chemicals
  • Doesn't keep hot/cold very long
