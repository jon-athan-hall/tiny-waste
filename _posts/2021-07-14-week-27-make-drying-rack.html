This week, while researching the environmental impact of our electricity system, I learned that my clothes dryer is the second highest energy consuming appliance in my home, after the refrigerator.

This is a very easy project that I was able to complete while six months pregnant. The materials cost less than $20.

What you'll need:

Materials

  • 2 6ft 1X4 boards. I used common wood from Home Depot, which cost roughly $3 per board.
  • 3 6ft 3/8" dowel rods
  • 4 1 1/2" corner braces with screws
  • 2 1 1/2" hinges with screws
  • 2 #10 hooks
  • 2 #210 eyes
  • 2 lengths of 2ft chain
  • 2 2" screws to attach drying rack to wall

Tools

  • Drill
  • 3/8" drill bit
  • Phillips bit
  • Pliars
  • Sandpaper or sanding block
  • Saw. Most home improvement stores will cut the boards for you and offer the use of a hand saw for dowel rods.
  1. {% include image-source-set.html image="27-electricity/1-cut-wood.jpg" alt="" %}

    Cut your 1x4s into 2 3ft pieces and 3 2ft pieces. Cut each dowel in half, making 6 2ft pieces.

  2. {% include image-source-set.html image="27-electricity/2-measure-holes.jpg" alt="" %}

    On the wide side of one of your 3ft boards, measure about 5 1/16" from the end.

  3. {% include image-source-set.html image="27-electricity/3-mark-all-holes.jpg" alt="" %}

    Continue to measure and mark every 5 1/16" down the length of the board. You should have a total of six marks. This will not be a perfectly even splitXXXX, but as long as your dowels are evenly spaced, it's fine.

  4. {% include image-source-set.html image="27-electricity/4-drill-holes.jpg" alt="" %}

    Stack your 3ft boards and using a 3/8" drill bit, drill holes at every mark.

  5. {% include image-source-set.html image="27-electricity/5-sand.jpg" alt="" %}

    Give everything a quick sanding, especially where you've just drilled the holes.

  6. {% include image-source-set.html image="27-electricity/6-attach-top.jpg" alt="" %}

    Attach the end of one 3ft board to the side of one 2ft board at a 90 degree angle. The screws should go into the 2ft board.

  7. {% include image-source-set.html image="27-electricity/7-add-corner-braces.jpg" alt="" %}

    As you attach the boards, add your corner braces.

  8. {% include image-source-set.html image="27-electricity/8-attach-bottom.jpg" alt="" %}

    Attach the next 2ft board to the other end of your 3ft board. Add corner brace.

  9. {% include image-source-set.html image="27-electricity/9-attach-side.jpg" alt="" %}

    Make sure your holes align properly, then attach the other 3ft board, completing the frame. Your frame should not be wider that 2ft. Add corner braces

  10. {% include image-source-set.html image="27-electricity/10-detach-side.jpg" alt="" %}

    Unscrew and detach the 3ft board you just added, leaving the corner braces attached to the top and bottom pieces.

  11. {% include image-source-set.html image="27-electricity/11-add-dowels.jpg" alt="" %}

    Insert a dowel into each hole. You may need to take them with a hammer.

  12. {% include image-source-set.html image="27-electricity/12-reattach-side.jpg" alt="" %}

    Reattach the other 3ft side pieces, carefully lining up each dowel and tapping into place.

  13. {% include image-source-set.html image="27-electricity/13-reattach-corner-braces.jpg" alt="" %}

    Reattach the corner braces.

  14. {% include image-source-set.html image="27-electricity/14-add-hinges.jpg" alt="" %}

    Line up your final 2ft board with the bottom 2ft piece and attach with hinges.

  15. {% include image-source-set.html image="27-electricity/15-add-screw-eyes.jpg" alt="" %}

    On your top piece, add a screw eyes just inside the frame. Use pliars to tighten.

  16. {% include image-source-set.html image="27-electricity/16-add-chain.jpg" alt="" %}

    Add one length of chain to screw eye. You may need pliars to loosen and tighten the screw eye.

  17. {% include image-source-set.html image="27-electricity/17-attach-other-chain.jpg" alt="" %}

    Repeat steps 15 and 16 on the other end of your top 2ft board.

  18. {% include image-source-set.html image="27-electricity/18-screw-hooks.jpg" alt="" %}

    Now your drying rack is ready to be mounted. Attach your screw eyes to the wall, making sure you drill into the studs. They should be level.

  19. {% include image-source-set.html image="27-electricity/19-screw-into-wall.jpg" alt="" %}

    Center and rest the top of your fram on the screw hooks, then screw the bottom-most panel into the wall. Again, making sure you are drilling into studs.

  20. {% include image-source-set.html image="27-electricity/20-finished-rack.jpg" alt="" %}

    Your drying rack is now complete and can be let down at various angles by adjusting the chain.
