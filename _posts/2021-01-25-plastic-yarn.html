  1. {% include image-source-set.html image="4-plastic-bags/1-flatten-bag.jpg" %}

    Flatten your plastic bag.

  2. {% include image-source-set.html image="4-plastic-bags/2-cut-bottom.jpg" %}

    Fold the bag in half hot dog style and cut the bottom seam off.

  3. {% include image-source-set.html image="4-plastic-bags/3-cut-top.jpg" %}

    With the bag still folded, cut the top handles off.

  4. {% include image-source-set.html image="4-plastic-bags/4-spread-tube.jpg" %}

    Spread the bag out to find a tube shape and flatten.

  5. {% include image-source-set.html image="4-plastic-bags/5-fold-up.jpg" %}

    Fold the closed edge up about an inch below the other closed edge.

  6. {% include image-source-set.html image="4-plastic-bags/6-cut-strips.jpg" %}

    Cut half inch strips starting from the fold and going just past the edge of the top layer.

  7. {% include image-source-set.html image="4-plastic-bags/7-cut-all.jpg" %}

    Continue cutting strips until you've reached the other end of the bag. They don't need to be perfect.

  8. {% include image-source-set.html image="4-plastic-bags/8-find-tube.jpg" %}

    Find your tube the same way you did in step 4.

  9. {% include image-source-set.html image="4-plastic-bags/9-find-spine.jpg" %}

    Put a piece of cardboard or anything to help keep your tube orderly. You should have what looks like a spine on top.

  10. {% include image-source-set.html image="4-plastic-bags/10-diagonal-cuts.jpg" %}

    Make diagonal cuts going from the right edge of your bottom strips, to the right edge of it's neighboring top strip.

  11. {% include image-source-set.html image="4-plastic-bags/11-roll-plarn.jpg" %}

    When all of your diagonal cuts are made, you should have one continuous strip. Roll it up as you would regular yarn.

  12. {% include image-source-set.html image="4-plastic-bags/12-finished-plarn.jpg" %}

    You've saved a bag from the landfill, now go forth and make something with your new plarn!
